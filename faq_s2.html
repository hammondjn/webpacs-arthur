General Information

What are Arthur and MOBIUS?

MOBIUS is a group (consortium) of over fifty libraries throughout the state of Missouri that shares resources so patrons may borrow books not owned by their local library.

ARTHUR is a cluster or group of Missouri libraries, within the MOBIUS library consortium. It consists of six libraries in the central Missouri area: Columbia College, Lincoln University, the Missouri State Library, Stephens College, Westminster College, and William Woods University.

What do I do if Stafford Library does not have the material I want? Can I get a book or a photocopied article from another library?

You can borrow books from any MOBIUS member library in person or by requesting the book through the catalog system. You can get books from outside the MOBIUS system by using Interlibrary loan.

While you cannot get photocopied articles from another library through the MOBIUS system, you can get them through Interlibrary Loan. Keep in mind that Interlibrary Loan can take anywhere from a week to a couple of months.

[back to top]

What is Interlibrary Loan?

Interlibray Loan is the service through which patrons may borrow and lend resources outside MOBIUS in order to provide patron access to materials not held locally. Contact the library for more information.

[back to top]

When I request a book through MOBIUS it asks for a campus ID number, what is my campus ID?

Your campus ID is your student ID number with the Columbia Collge Location added to the end of the number. The code for Columbia College is CC. For example: 5555555CC.

Can I go to another library and use their resources?

Yes, you can visit any MOBIUS member library, use their resources and check out books. A list of libraries is available at http://mobius.umsystem.edu/screens/libinfo.html. You can find the hours of operation on their home pages. There are a few things to keep in mind before you go:

  1. Take the your student ID. If your ID card is not a picture ID take a picture ID with you.
  2. Check with the Columbia College library before you go to make sure your account is up to date. If your account is blocked for overdue books, fines or any other reason, you will need to clear that up before you can check out books at another library.
  3. Books are the only items you can check out from another library. Nonbook items cannot be checked out from other libraries than your own.
  4. Some libraries may allow you to use their periodical collections, but may not allow you to use their databases. If you need to use audiovisual equipment or materials, periodicals, databases, or any other nonbook items check with the library in question before you go.

Does the online catalog contain full text journal articles?

No, the online catalog lists magazines, newspapers, and journals in the library. The catalog displays the location within the library and what issues of that publication the library owns.

How do I get full text journal articles?

Check with the library to find out what options you have. If the library does not have the online or print full text of the article you need, it may be able to get it for you through Interlibrary Loan. Keep in mind that this may be a long process often taking more than two weeks.

How do I renew books?
  • Go to http://arthur.missouri.edu/search~.
  • Scroll down to the bar that reads, "View your own library record (Renew items)."
  • Enter your name and ID number as requested on the form. Remember to add the location code to your ID number, eg: 99999999CC
  • Select the link that shows the number of items you currently have checked out.
  • You can choose to renew all items or renew selected items. If you are only renewing some of the items, click on the box to the left of the item you want to renew.
  • Click on the appropriate button: "Renew all" or "Renew selected items".
Important note: Not all items are renewable. Check the status of your item(s) after submitting your renewal command. If it renewed successfully a new due date will appear. If not, there will be a message showing the reason why not. Books from other institutions can take time to renew. Check back after a while to see if the renewal was successful. If you have questions or problems, please call or e-mail the circulation department at your library.

What is My Reading History?

You can keep track of what library items you have checked out and have checked out in the past by activating the "My Reading History" feature on your library account.

This service is free and optional.

To activate your Reading History:

Access your library account, then click on My Reading History and Opt In.

After you activate the Reading History, the system will keep track of and display a list of items you check out from that time forward. Items currently checked out at the time of signing up will not be listed. Please note: Items ordered through MOBIUS will not be included in your Reading History.

To delete items or turn off your Reading History:

You can delete any or all of the items in your Reading History at any time by using the Delete Marked or Delete All buttons. You can also turn off the Reading History feature all together by clicking the Opt Out button. Before doing so, however, you will need to delete all entries from your Reading History.

Stafford Library complies with the Columbia College Privacy Policy.
